⚖ Data Preservation vs Pipeline Safety — when a camera gamut exceeds 100% of a target colour space:

When a camera's native gamut is larger than the target colour space — for example a medium-format sensor whose green primary reaches well beyond sRGB — there is a fundamental choice between two philosophies at every stage of the raw processing pipeline.

Data Preservation keeps all captured colour information intact for as long as possible. Working in a wide-gamut space such as ProPhoto RGB or a proprietary camera space means no out-of-gamut values are discarded at the raw conversion stage. Highly saturated greens, cyans, or magentas that lie outside sRGB remain encoded and available for deliberate creative or editorial decisions downstream. The tradeoff is that values beyond the destination space can cause clipping artefacts, hue shifts, or rendering errors in software that assumes all values are within a standard gamut — a particular risk when handing off files to colour-unaware applications, broadcast pipelines, or web workflows.

Pipeline Safety converts early to the delivery colour space, ensuring that every value is in-gamut throughout the workflow. This eliminates the risk of out-of-range values being clipped differently at each subsequent application, produces consistent results across colour-unaware tools, and simplifies roundtrip fidelity. The cost is that any colour the sensor captured beyond the target gamut is permanently discarded at the conversion step — it cannot be recovered later.

In practice the choice is guided by intent: archival and retouching workflows favour data preservation (editing in ProPhoto or a wide proprietary space, converting only at final output); broadcast, web, and collaborative review workflows favour pipeline safety (early conversion to sRGB or Rec.709 to ensure consistency across all viewing contexts). The gamut maps in this tool visualise exactly what is at stake — the area outside the dashed target triangle, but inside the solid native triangle, represents the colour data that must either be preserved, mapped, or discarded.

📐 A larger ForwardMatrix2 does not necessarily mean better final images. It means:

1. The manufacturer assumes downstream processing (your raw converter + LUT) can handle extreme values without artefacts.
2. The manufacturer prioritises potential maximum saturation over pipeline safety.
3. The hardware (lens + CFA) actually supports that gamut.

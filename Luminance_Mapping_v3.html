Analysis

What this tool adds over the xy chromaticity tool: It computes the full RGB→XYZ matrix from each colour space's primaries and white point, converts to CIELAB (perceptually uniform), and reports ΔL* (lightness shift) alongside Δhue° and Δchroma. ΔL* measures how much a primary's luminance contribution changes — a pure xy analysis cannot see this. Green typically dominates luminance (Y≈0.71–0.72 for sRGB/ProPhoto) so green primary shifts carry the largest lightness impact.

Lab axes: L* = lightness (0 = black, 100 = white) · a* = green↔red axis · b* = blue↔yellow axis · C* = chroma = √(a*²+b*²) · h° = hue angle = atan2(b*,a*) in degrees. All deltas are source-minus-target; ΔL* positive means source primary is lighter than target primary.

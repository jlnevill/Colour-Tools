Analysis

Mapping strategies: Hard Clip — each primary clipped independently to target boundary along WP ray · Compress — uniform scale of whole triangle to fit; shape preserved · Hue-Preserve — each primary moved to boundary at constant hue angle · Chroma Map — proportional boundary-to-boundary remap: r_mapped = r_src × (r_tgt_bnd / r_src_bnd); preserves relative chroma position (ICC perceptual analogue) · Rel. Colorimetric — shifts the source white point to the target white point (pure WP translation in xy), preserving all xy offsets from the WP exactly; any primaries still outside the target gamut after the shift are hard-clipped to the boundary. Models ICC relative colorimetric intent: colours that match under the source illuminant are reproduced to match under the target illuminant.

⚖ Source > Target area means colour data loss at conversion. The area inside the solid source triangle but outside the dashed target triangle represents colours that must be mapped or clipped. Hard Clip and Hue-Preserve leave in-gamut primaries untouched; Compress and Chroma Map rescale the entire triangle. Compress preserves triangle shape; Chroma Map preserves relative chroma position within the gamut.
